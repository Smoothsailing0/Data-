# Public Management Writing Skills

This folder contains Chinese academic writing skills for public management, public administration, digital governance, rural governance, grassroots governance, and ethnic-region governance research.

## Included skills

- `public-management-academic-writing-zh`
  - For CSSCI/C journal papers, abstracts, introductions, literature review sections, research design, results interpretation, discussion, and conclusion writing.
  - Core idea: write with function-aware structure first, then paragraph logic, then sentence-level clarity.

- `public-management-phd-ethnic-digital-governance-anti-aigc-zh`
  - For PhD dissertation writing on ethnic-region rural digital governance.
  - Core idea: preserve doctoral-level density, field context, mechanism explanation, and authorial judgment while reducing template-like AI writing.

## Design principle

These skills treat academic writing as argument construction rather than simple polishing. Before drafting, the writer should identify what each section or paragraph does in the whole paper: introducing a problem, extending literature, defining a concept, entering a case, explaining a method, interpreting results, or closing an argument.

The goal is to avoid mechanical paragraph openings and produce writing that is clearer, more grounded, and closer to Chinese public management scholarship.
